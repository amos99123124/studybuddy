To find the derivative of the function \( f(x) = 6x^3 - 9x + 4 \), we will apply the power rule of differentiation. The power rule states that if \( f(x) = ax^n \), then \( f'(x) = anx^{n-1} \).

Let's differentiate each term of the function \( f(x) \) separately:

1. Differentiate \( 6x^3 \):

\[ \frac{d}{dx}(6x^3) = 6 \cdot 3x^{3-1} = 18x^2 \]

2. Differentiate \( -9x \):

\[ \frac{d}{dx}(-9x) = -9 \cdot 1x^{1-1} = -9 \]

3. Differentiate the constant \( 4 \):

\[ \frac{d}{dx}(4) = 0 \]

Now, combine the derivatives of each term to get the derivative of the entire function:

\[ f'(x) = 18x^2 - 9 \]

Therefore, the derivative of \( f(x) = 6x^3 - 9x + 4 \) is:

\[ f'(x) = 18x^2 - 9 \]
