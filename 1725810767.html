Let's break down the problem step-by-step.

The problem states that the sculptor builds a small-scale model of a terrapin and discovers that it requires 2 kg of bronze to cast it. The final statue is supposed to be 5 times as large as the model in each dimension.

We need to calculate how much bronze the sculptor will need to make the final statue.

First, let's understand how the volume of the statue changes when the dimensions are scaled up.

When each dimension of an object is scaled by a factor of \( k \), the volume scales by a factor of \( k^3 \).

In this case, the dimensions are scaled by a factor of 5, so the volume will scale by:

\( 5^3 \)

\( 5^3 = 125 \)

This means the final statue will be 125 times the volume of the small-scale model.

Since the small-scale model requires 2 kg of bronze, the final statue will require:

\( 2 \, \text{kg} \times 125 \)

\( 2 \times 125 = 250 \, \text{kg} \)

So, the sculptor will need 250 kg of bronze to cast the final statue.
