Let's solve the given linear equation in two variables: \(2x + 4y - 31 = 123\).

First, we solve for \(x\). To do this, we isolate \(x\) on one side of the equation:

\[ 2x + 4y - 31 = 123 \]

Add 31 to both sides of the equation:

\[ 2x + 4y - 31 + 31 = 123 + 31 \]

\[ 2x + 4y = 154 \]

Next, we solve for \(x\) by isolating it. Subtract \(4y\) from both sides:

\[ 2x = 154 - 4y \]

Divide both sides by 2:

\[ x = \frac{154 - 4y}{2} \]

Simplify the right-hand side:

\[ x = 77 - 2y \]

So, \(x\) in terms of \(y\) is:

\[ x = 77 - 2y \]

Now, let's solve for \(y\). To do this, we isolate \(y\) on one side of the equation:

Starting from the simplified equation:

\[ 2x + 4y = 154 \]

Subtract \(2x\) from both sides:

\[ 4y = 154 - 2x \]

Divide both sides by 4:

\[ y = \frac{154 - 2x}{4} \]

Simplify the right-hand side:

\[ y = \frac{154}{4} - \frac{2x}{4} \]

\[ y = 38.5 - 0.5x \]

So, \(y\) in terms of \(x\) is:

\[ y = 38.5 - 0.5x \]

Thus, the solutions for \(x\) and \(y\) in terms of each other are:

\[ x = 77 - 2y \]

and

\[ y = 38.5 - 0.5x \]
